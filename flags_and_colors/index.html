Colors in Flags

By Cynthia Seidel

There are countless flags in the world. Every geographical region has at least one, often depending on whether the region agrees on who governs it. However, a flag symbolizes more than just a city, state or country. The colors stand for certain values with which the region wants to present itself to the outside world. The composition often reveals something about the history of the region. A story about the use of national colors.

Counting the 193 member states of the United Nations, the Vatican City and 15 territories whose statehood is disputed (such as Palestine, Taiwan or Tibet), there are a total of 209 country flags. And even though there are countless possible combinations, some flags look confusingly similar - even without one of the two countries actually being colonized by the other in the past.

Meaningful Colors

Let's take a closer look at which continent is the most colorful. To simplify matters, Antarctica is regarded separately from the other six continents. But eventhough Antarctica does not belong to any country nor has a permanent population, the continent or country (or landmass?) has its own flag.

What colors is the Antarctic flag?

According to Matador Network the flag was designed by the american journalist Evan Townsend in 2018, when they were working at one of the research stations. The flag's colors are not only reflecting the colors so characteristic of the continent, is also representing the unpolitical nature of Antarctica since the shade of dark blue is flag is not featured on any country's flag. The two stips – one BLUE , one WHITE – are therefore representing the long nights and long days in Antarctica, whereas the white diamond in the center represents mountains and icebergs, and below, like a mirror image of the peak, is a compass arrow pointing south.

The colors GREEN and YELLOW are also being used in at least three/ nine countries per continent. However they are most common in Africa where they occur more than twice as often as on the other continents. This comes as no surprise as the color Yellow symbolizes wealth and status in Africa, while Green – according to John Elmore – "represents the land and abundant natural resources found across the continent. It signifies the importance of preserving Africa's environment and ensuring sustainable development".

Colors that are rather rare are mostly colors where the meaning of the color itself differs. E.g. BROWN is featured three time each in Europe and South America. In latter it represents the feelings of home, warmth, and comfort, offering a sense of safety and security whereas in Europe the color was associated with barbarians or lower classes.

Colors that are even more rare than the one just metioned are PURPLE, ORANGE, GRAYand GOLD. Purple in fact is only ever used once in the whole wide world: in North America, Dominica
